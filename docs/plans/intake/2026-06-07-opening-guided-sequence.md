# Opening guided sequence

**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Filed by:** Sihl
**Project:** chairfighter
**Card chain root:** KOQP

## What the friend asked for

"I need a better way of giving information to the player especially at the start. it's very confusing what to do or what buttons to press, which might mean not enough instructions or not enough visual feedback"

## What we agreed on

The start of the game should teach the player through a short guided sequence that happens naturally in the first playable area, not in a separate tutorial room and not through a big wall of text. The sequence should cover movement, attacking, interacting, and any immediate progression action the player needs. Each prompt should disappear as soon as the player successfully does the requested action, so the feedback is immediate and the screen does not stay cluttered.

The opening area may need more safe space before the first enemy. The first enemy can be visible as a future threat, but it should not pressure the player while they are learning; separating the enemy area behind a jump/crossing or spawning it later are both acceptable.

This request is about early instruction and visual feedback, not a full dialogue/tutorial system, final art, or major redesign of combat.

## Acceptance criteria

- The opening area teaches the player with a short guided sequence in the actual first playable level.
- The sequence prompts the player through movement, attacking, interacting, and the first progression action needed to continue.
- Each prompt disappears as soon as its action is completed.
- The start area gives the player enough safe room before the first enemy or boss pressure begins.
- Any visible early enemy is not an immediate threat during the basic prompts.
- Instructional UI/visuals use placeholder text/shapes and preserve controller-first input with keyboard support.
- The existing silent protagonist, melee combat, and MVP flow are preserved.
